What Inspired me to Pursue Data Analytics

I enjoy watching baseball and I am a huge fan of the Mets. What I enjoy more than just watching the Mets, is listening to the Mets broadcasters, such as Gary Cohen. His ability to articulate the significance of what has occurred in game, or what could occur, while framing it as something that has never happened in franchise history, is an art that has drawn me to the world of data analytics. He does a good job at getting me excited, or passionate about a player's accomplishments, and their battles in every inning all throughout the game. I love his passion and his ability to use data to tell such an engaging story that is inviting to both his peers, as well as the listeners. Every game has its own storyline, and Gary Cohen does a great job at piecing that story together.

Cleaning Data in SQL
using SSMS

With the NashvilleHousing Dataset, I use SQL to get familiar with my data before the cleaning process can begin. I begin the cleaning process by first converting columns to the correct datatype, then splitting columns into new appropriate columns, deleting duplicates, and finally deleting the unused columns. This project has taught me that it's important to get to know your data before you can start deleting it. Using Row_Number and the Partition By functions together helped me in figuring out how I wanted to delete the duplicate rows.
